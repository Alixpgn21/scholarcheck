# BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding

**Authors:** Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, Kristina Toutanova
**Year:** 2018
**Venue:** NAACL
**DOI:** 10.18653/v1/N19-1423
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805

## Abstract

We introduce BERT, a new method for pre-training language representations from raw text that can be fine-tuned with just one additional output layer to achieve state-of-the-art results on a wide range of tasks. Unlike recent language representation work which looks at the context from left to right or right to left, BERT trains deep bidirectional representations from unlabeled text by jointly conditioning on both left and right context. As a result, the pre-trained BERT model can be fine-tuned with minimal task-specific architecture changes and can excel at a large suite of natural language understanding tasks including question answering, sentiment analysis, named entity recognition, coreference resolution, paraphrase detection, and text classification. BERT obtains new state-of-the-art results on eleven NLU tasks, including pushing the GLUE benchmark to 80.5% (7.7% absolute improvement), MultiNLI accuracy to 86.7% (4.6% absolute improvement), and SQuAD v1.1 question answering Test F1 to 93.2 (which surpasses human performance by 2.0%).
