# Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners

**Authors:** Alec Radford, Jeffrey Wu, Rewon Child, David Luan, Dario Amodei, Ilya Sutskever
**Year:** 2019
**Venue:** OpenAI Blog
**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.1901.11490
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.11490

## Abstract

We demonstrate that language models begin to learn these tasks without any explicit supervision when trained on a new dataset of Internet text assembled as described in the WebText. The capacity of the language model appears to be the key factor determining the success of zero-shot task transfer and increasing it improves performance in a roughly predictable way across tasks. We build a large-scale, unsupervised language model called GPT-2. The model demonstrates strong performance on language understanding and generation tasks. This work provides evidence that language models are capable of solving diverse natural language tasks without task-specific fine-tuning. The remarkable capability of language models to perform zero-shot learning suggests they may be learning useful representations of natural language.
