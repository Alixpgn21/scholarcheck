# Efficient Estimation of Word Representations in Vector Space

**Authors:** Tomas Mikolov, Ilya Sutskever, Kai Chen, Gregory Corrado, Jeffrey Dean
**Year:** 2013
**Venue:** ICLR
**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.1301.3781
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.3781

## Abstract

We propose two novel model architectures for computing continuous vector representations of words from very large data sets. The quality of these representations is measured in a word similarity task, and the results are compared to the previously best performing techniques based on different types of neural networks. We observe that not all word pairs can be graded with human judgments, and not all model architectures equally well capture human-perceived similarities. Human-perceived quality of word representations, commonly denoted by the word similarity task, is an important aspect for operations of word embeddings. We show that the skip-gram model and a method for learning phrase representations as single token embeddings substantially outperform all previous word embedding techniques on a word similarity task and that these vectors provide state-of-the-art performance in our test set for measuring syntactic and semantic word similarities.
