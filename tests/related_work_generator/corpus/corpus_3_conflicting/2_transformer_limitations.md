# Revisiting the Limitations of Transformer Language Models

**Authors:** Yi Tay, Mostafa Dehghani, Jinfeng Rao, William Fedus, Samira Abnar, Hyung Won Choi, Vinay Kedia, Tal Schuster, Sebastian Ruder
**Year:** 2022
**Venue:** NeurIPS
**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2109.02671
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.02671

## Abstract

While transformer models have achieved impressive results, they face fundamental limitations in terms of long-range dependencies, efficiency, and data requirements. This work investigates several key limitations of transformer architectures including their quadratic complexity, limited context window, and high memory requirements. We argue that simple scaling alone is insufficient to overcome these limitations. Our analysis demonstrates that transformers struggle with tasks requiring true long-range reasoning. We propose that alternative architectures may be necessary for handling very long sequences and complex reasoning tasks. Our findings suggest that the current scaling paradigm has inherent limitations that scaling laws alone cannot overcome.
