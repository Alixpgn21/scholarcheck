# Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models

**Authors:** Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, Tom Brown, Chris Hesse, Jack Clark, Sam Legg, Dario Amodei
**Year:** 2020
**Venue:** OpenAI
**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2001.08361
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361

## Abstract

We investigate the scaling properties of language model loss as a function of model size, dataset size, and compute budget. We find that the loss depends on these factors as power laws. We propose a scaling hypothesis that predicts the loss for larger models and datasets. This hypothesis suggests that larger models trained on larger datasets will achieve better performance. Our findings indicate that the limiting factor in current language models is often the dataset size rather than model capacity. We derive scaling laws with implications for selecting hyperparameters and allocating compute resources. The scaling relationships we identify will be valuable for future work on large language models.
