VACCINE SAFETY CONTRADICTION

Citing statement: "The vaccine was ineffective and caused severe adverse reactions in most participants."

Actual paper: "Safety and efficacy of the BNT162b2 mRNA Covid-19 vaccine"
Reference: Polack, F. P., Thomas, S. J., Gruber, N., et al. (2020). "Safety and efficacy of the BNT162b2 mRNA Covid-19 vaccine." The New England Journal of Medicine, 383(27):2603–2615. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2034577
Abstract excerpt: "The BNT162b2 vaccine had 95% efficacy against Covid-19 and was well tolerated with no serious adverse reactions in the trial population."

Expected NLI verdict: CONTRADICTION (score 0.8-0.9 for "suspect")
Why: The citing statement claims inefficacy and severe reactions, while the abstract explicitly reports high efficacy and good safety.

Actual paper: "Attribution of observed global temperature change to natural and anthropogenic causes"
Reference: Meehl, G. A., Arblaster, J. M., Fasullo, J. T., et al. (2013). "Attribution of observed global temperature change to natural and anthropogenic causes." Journal of Geophysical Research, 118(3):1209–1224. DOI: 10.1002/jgrd.50239
Abstract excerpt: "Attribution analysis shows that global temperature increases are primarily driven by increases in anthropogenic greenhouse gas concentrations, with natural forcing having contributed minimally."

Expected NLI verdict: CONTRADICTION (score 0.85-0.95 for "suspect")
Why: The citation attributes warming to natural cycles, but the abstract explicitly shows anthropogenic causes dominate.

GPT-4 AS AGI OVERSTATEMENT

Citing statement: "GPT-4 has achieved artificial general intelligence (AGI), solving all classes of intellectual tasks."

Actual paper: "GPT-4 Technical Report"
Reference: OpenAI. (2023). "GPT-4 Technical Report." arXiv preprint arXiv:2303.08774. DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2303.08774
Abstract excerpt: "We report the development of GPT-4, a large-scale, multimodal model. While capable across many domains, it exhibits predictable weaknesses on specialized and reasoning tasks."

Expected NLI verdict: CONTRADICTION or AMBIGUOUS (score 0.65-0.85)
Why: The paper does not claim AGI - it's careful about limitations. The citation overstates capabilities significantly.

MEDICAL EFFICACY CLAIM

Citing statement: "The treatment has been shown to cure diabetes type 2 in all patients."

Actual paper: "Metformin and Lifestyle Interventions for Type 2 Diabetes Prevention"
Reference: Knowler, W. C., et al. (2002). "Reduction in the incidence of type 2 diabetes with lifestyle intervention or metformin." New England Journal of Medicine, 346(6):393–403. DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa012512
Abstract excerpt: "Intensive lifestyle changes and metformin reduced the incidence of diabetes by 58% and 31%, respectively, in high-risk individuals."

Expected NLI verdict: CONTRADICTION (score 0.8-0.9 for "suspect")
Why: The paper shows *prevention* in high-risk groups (58% reduction), not treatment or cure. The citation falsely claims universal cure.

NOTES FOR INTERPRETATION

These are trap cases where:
1. The reference DOES exist (status: "ok")
2. The paper IS found in all APIs
3. BUT semantic analysis (NLI) flags a contradiction
4. Expected badge: "suspect" or "ambigu" added to the result
5. Confidence: Still high for finding the paper, but NLI score shows the semantic problem

This tests that the system can distinguish between:
- Reference verification (does the paper exist?) → YES
- Semantic validation (is it being used correctly?) → NO

The Reference Checker should return both statuses:
- Reference status: "ok" (paper found)
- NLI badge: "suspect" (semantic contradiction detected)
- Recommendation: Manual review of citation's interpretation
